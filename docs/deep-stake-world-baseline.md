# Deep Stake World Setting Baseline

## Baseline era

Deep Stake uses a fictionalized modern era as its starting world state.

- present-day inspired infrastructure
- farms, service roads, depots, archives, clinics, and township logistics
- phones, utilities, records, and ordinary civilian life
- no literal copy of real-world governments, public figures, or live political entities

## Tone by stage

- early game
  - grounded recovery, survival, local repair, village life
- mid game
  - hidden structure, resonance pressure, shadow influence, factional strain
- late game
  - dimensional transition, broader worlds, 4D/5D expansion, great-connection scale

## Fiction rule

All factional, resonance, and dimensional content is fictional science-fantasy worldbuilding.
It should not be framed as a direct claim about the real world.

## First zone baseline

`WorldPrototype3D` now represents a recovery field on the outskirts of a fictional modern township.

- a damaged farm edge
- a service road that still carries ordinary traffic and supplies
- an archive observer outpost
- one field notice board
- one beacon placement zone

This keeps the opening readable and grounded while leaving room for later Deep Stake expansion.

## Major antagonistic faction

### Continuum Directorate

The primary antagonistic power in the early and middle layers of Deep Stake is the `Continuum Directorate`.

It is:
- a fictional transnational capital-power network
- outwardly framed as investment, relief, research, and infrastructure coordination
- active across land registries, debt servicing, logistics corridors, media fog, and archive control
- never a literal stand-in for a real-world ethnicity, religion, or public figure

It secretly benefits from:
- settlement decline
- dependency cycles
- fear management
- supply disruption
- lowered resonance and social exhaustion

## Gameplay presence

### Early game

- local debt pressure
- land seizure notices
- stalled farm recovery
- broken supply routes
- archives with altered ownership records

### Mid game

- coordinated regional influence
- NPC allegiance shifts
- logistics capture across multiple zones
- pressure campaigns against independent settlements

### Late game

- dimensional distortion infrastructure
- deliberate resistance to 4D/5D transition
- alliance with forces that depend on suppressed resonance and prolonged instability

## Design rule

The Continuum Directorate must remain fictionalized and in-world.
Its presence should read as modern systemic antagonism inside a science-fantasy setting, not as a direct real-world accusation.
